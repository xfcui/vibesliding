# Abstract: VibeSliding — From Idea to 60-Slide Deck in Under an Hour

## Presentation Abstract

What if a single paragraph were enough to produce a presentation you'd be proud to give? We present **VibeSliding**, an autonomous pipeline that turns a one-paragraph idea into a publication-grade 60-slide deck — verified citations, full speaker notes, custom visuals — in under 60 minutes at $4.73 in API costs, versus 94 minutes of post-editing with single-shot AI tools.

The system rests on two deceptively simple principles. **Draft-then-refine** means every stage runs twice: the first pass explores the space, the second hardens the output with verified metrics and inline citations. **Orchestrate commodity tools** means no custom models or bespoke renderers — just three platforms composed into something greater: Cursor for IDE orchestration, OpenRouter for 200+ models, and Valyu for citation-grounded research. Four stages each produce a transparent, inspectable artifact: Deep Research grows a 12-citation base to 38 verified sources; Content Generation emits a fully specified per-slide outline with bullets, `[Visual:]` art-direction, and `[Speech:]` scripts; Image Generation renders 4 parallel variants per slide via a contact-sheet selection workflow; and Human Curation assembles the final PDF from the best picks. The orchestration layer compounds; the models are interchangeable.

VibeSliding is open-source (MIT), 100% Python, and available at `github.com/xfcui/vibesliding`.
